New York City Emergency Management releases Hurricane Evacuation Zones (aka "Hurricane Zones", aka "HEZ") annually, usually in late spring.  Hurricane Evacuation Zones are built from collections of [Atomic-Polygons](./Atomic-Polygons.md)

Zone 0 is the water and then zones 1 through 7 are on land.  Zone X is not a zone, it is a placeholder for territory without a zone.

It is hard to predict which source below will deliver the most current hurricane zones.  In the offseason, hurricane-wise, all will likely be synchronized.  As the hurricane season begins the zones will likely be delivered from NYC Emergency Management and flow out to the other end points.

## Geocoders 

New York City geocoders return hurricane zones for an address.

* GOAT (http://a030-goat.nyc.gov/goat/Default.aspx)
* Geosupport (https://www1.nyc.gov/site/planning/data-maps/open-data/dwn-gde-home.page)
* Geoclient (https://api.cityofnewyork.us/geoclient/v1/doc)
