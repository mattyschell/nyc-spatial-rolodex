The New York City Department of City Planning publishes transit zones.  Transit Zones are a new geography designated in 2024.

See also [[Greater-Transit-Zones]]

### New York City Department of City Planning

"Transit Zone boundaries are mapped to encompass neighborhoods that are dense, proximate to public transportation options, and where car ownership rates are lowest. Within the Transit Zone, no offstreet parking spaces are required to be built for Income Restricted Housing Units (also referred to as IRHU) and Affordable Independent Residences for Seniors (also referred to as AIRS), and discretionary actions allow for the removal of parking spaces that were previously required, or the reduction of spaces that would normally need to be built for certain other housing types."
